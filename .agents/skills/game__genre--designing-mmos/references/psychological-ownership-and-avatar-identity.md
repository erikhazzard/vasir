# Psychological Ownership and Avatar Identity

Use this when designing avatar systems, housing, cosmetics, collections, professions, homes, maps, crafted goods, AI companions, or legacy.

## Core prior rewrite

Bad default: identity systems are cosmetics and monetization surfaces.

Replacement: persistent worlds retain players when part of the self appears to live in the world. Avatar, home, tools, profession, maps, social records, and authored history become psychological property.

## Ownership channels

| Channel | “Mine/ours” feeling | Design surfaces |
|---|---|---|
| avatar | this is me here | body, face, animation, fashion, name, portrait, emotes |
| home/place | I belong somewhere | housing, districts, venue history, neighborhood memory |
| tools/items | this has my history | named gear, crafted provenance, repairs, inscriptions |
| profession | this is my role in society | public craft record, commission history, reputation |
| map/knowledge | I know this world | annotations, routes, discoveries, intel archive |
| institution | we built this | guild hall, records, charters, monuments |
| story/history | I was there | chronicles, titles, event traces, public records |

## Design questions

1. What can a player change that persists?
2. What can they display without pay-to-status confusion?
3. What proof says “this was made/held/discovered by me/us”?
4. What survives a patch without freezing the world?
5. What is the player afraid to lose because it represents self, not power?

## Watchouts

- Paid cosmetics can contaminate earned identity if channels are not separated.
- Ownership without portability can become hostage retention.
- Total malleability can make identity weightless.
- AI companions can deepen identity, but should not replace human relatedness.
