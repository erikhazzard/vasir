# Classic EverQuest Source Spine

Use this for claim discipline when invoking Classic EQ or Luclin. Keep the design lesson separate from what the source actually proves.

| Claim | Source support | Limit | Design implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shadows of Luclin was the third EQ expansion and added Luclin, 24 zones, Vah Shir, Beastlord, Alternate Advancement, mounts, and Bazaar. | Official EverQuest Progression Primer, Nov 20 2018. | Retrospective official summary, not player sentiment. | Luclin was a major system/lore/tech expansion, not a tiny patch. |
| EQ did not obviously collapse immediately because of Luclin. | SOE press release, Mar 5 2003: more than 430,000 active subscribers after Luclin and Planes of Power. | Press release numbers are corporate and not a full cohort analysis. | Treat “Luclin killed EQ” as design-texture claim, not simple subscriber fact. |
| Bazaar was a convenience upgrade but changed the social character of trade. | Game Studies interview with Kevin McPherson: Bazaar more convenient; player markets had haggling, barter, personal touch; he met people through trading. | One developer perspective, not universal player consensus. | Market automation should preserve seller identity, bargaining, and market-place memory. |
| Nexus/Bazaar reduced perceived world size and made some old travel content/social spots obsolete. | Game Studies interview with Kevin McPherson; Taylor, Play Between Worlds, describes Nexus/NPC/self-service porting and later PoP books reducing reliance on player porters and killing regional gathering spots. | PoP intensified the travel centralization after Luclin. | Travel convenience needs replacement memory: last-mile routes, porter/guide roles, staging hubs, route identity. |
| EC/GFay-style trade hubs existed before Bazaar. | Wikibooks summary: before Shadows of Luclin, players gathered in East Commonlands, Greater Faydark, or North Freeport, auctioned to zone, and manually completed trades. | Secondary/community source. | Player-created markets can be more socially alive than designed service clusters. |
| Bazaar implemented automatic trader/search behavior. | TAKP archived patch notes: /bazaar search window and /trader vendor window in Bazaar near Nexus/Shadowhaven. | Archive, not original live patch page. | Search and asynchronous selling solve pain, but risk price-row anonymity. |
| Classic EQ established or strongly popularized holy-trinity-style role thinking. | Meaningful Play paper says EverQuest established the holy trinity as a design concept in MMOs. | Paper is interpretive and has scope limits. | Do not stop at trinity; Classic EQ also had pullers, CC, slow, snare, port, bind, rez, track, guide, and rescue roles. |

Design use:

```text
source fact → supported claim → design interpretation → modern translation
```

Never use a source as proof of nostalgia. Use it to identify the social function that should be preserved or deliberately discarded.
