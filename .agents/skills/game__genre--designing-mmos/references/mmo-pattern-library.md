# MMO Pattern Library

Use this to diagnose recurring MMO social-system failures. Each pattern names a failure, the bad default, the expert replacement, and the key diagnostic question.

## Recognition Debt

Players help each other, but the game forgets the relationship.

- **Bad default:** reward the action and move on.
- **Damage:** useful strangers remain disposable.
- **Replacement:** recent ally memory, commendations, play-again prompts, leader follow, guild/squad discovery, repeat-contact surfacing.
- **Question:** who helped me tonight, and can I find them tomorrow?

## Role Coercion Threshold

Legal play becomes socially illegal when a role is scarce, visible, and group-critical.

- **Bad default:** allow flexible builds but reveal intent only after failure.
- **Damage:** hybrid/off-meta players are blamed for not fulfilling implied obligations.
- **Replacement:** role declaration, build intent, story/prog/farm/speed tags, learning-mode labels, honest matchmaking, encounters that value utility.
- **Question:** will the group punish a player for using a build the game appears to endorse?

## Useful Friction

Friction earns its place only when it creates attention, choice, reciprocity, danger, profession, market identity, or memory.

- **Bad default:** treat friction as either community magic or UX sin.
- **Damage:** nostalgia delay or sterile menu optimization.
- **Replacement:** keep friction that creates decisions and memory; remove friction that creates silent waiting.
- **Question:** does this delay create a decision, profession, relationship, or just a tab-out?

## Social Decay by Optimization

Throughput rises while repeat interaction falls.

- **Bad default:** optimize queue time, travel time, transaction time, and completion rate in isolation.
- **Damage:** the game gets smoother while recognition disappears.
- **Replacement:** add memory surfaces back into optimized flows.
- **Question:** what relationship did this convenience remove, and how do we replace the useful part without restoring the tedious part?

## Repair Deficit

The game creates obligations but does not help players recover when they fail.

- **Bad default:** provide death, wipe, or failure evidence without social scaffolding.
- **Damage:** blame, churn, support-role loss, leader burnout.
- **Replacement:** group-pattern summaries, retry paths, transparent policies, role clarity, regroup support, return paths.
- **Question:** after failure, does the system produce another attempt or a scapegoat?

## Guild Shell Without Squad Unit

The game supports guild identity but not the small groups that actually play.

- **Bad default:** guild chat, bank, ranks, roster.
- **Damage:** large groups become anonymous; small groups collapse under absence; officers become unpaid schedulers.
- **Replacement:** squads, event calendars, role needs, onboarding, substitution pools, delegation, cross-squad rituals.
- **Question:** how many members have played with more than one squad this month?

## Leader Bargain

A leader accepts blame in exchange for agency.

- **Bad default:** reward only victory or give leaders opaque control.
- **Damage:** tyrants, burnout, silent populations, blame concentration, exclusion of learners.
- **Replacement:** pings, markers, ready checks, subgroup assignment, transparent reasons, leader follow, session-quality rewards.
- **Question:** does the game make leadership easier, fairer, and more repeatable?

## Bank-Standing Social Machine

A storage node becomes a social stage when route reset, trade, risk recovery, profession services, and repeated contact overlap.

- **Bad default:** treat banks/storage as private UI.
- **Damage:** economic and profession activity becomes invisible, transactional, and placeless.
- **Replacement:** bank-stage neighborhoods, live commissions, seller/crafter identity, bounded trade chatter, nearby routes, and visible services.
- **Question:** after the transaction, is there still a reason to notice another human?

## Profession Loop Identity

Noncombat skills become memorable when production is visible, repeated, and useful.

- **Bad default:** make skilling a private XP spreadsheet.
- **Damage:** miners, fishers, smiths, cooks, crafters, merchants, scouts, and couriers never become social identities.
- **Replacement:** public resource routes, work orders, profession hubs, visible service history, group skilling, route mastery, and expert return paths.
- **Question:** where does the player become known for a profession?

## Risk-Gradient Frontier

Danger creates memory when players knowingly cross a spatial threshold and understand the stakes.

- **Bad default:** make risk either everywhere, hidden, or purely opt-in through menus.
- **Damage:** fear becomes confusion or resentment instead of story.
- **Replacement:** visible frontiers, staging edges, stake preview, scouting, commitment/escape windows, loss explanation, and trophies.
- **Question:** what is the modern Wilderness edge?

## Anti-Automation Friction Trap

A system tries to fight automation by taxing legitimate play.

- **Bad default:** add fatigue, captcha, interruption, or chore friction to the core loop.
- **Damage:** honest players suffer while automation adapts.
- **Replacement:** protect economic trust through telemetry, system incentives, resource design, and market structure rather than player-loop punishment.
- **Question:** who pays the cost: the automation, or the honest player?

## Scarcity Myth

A rare item becomes a lasting social symbol.

- **Bad default:** let one-time scarcity happen accidentally.
- **Damage:** permanent wealth distortion, status confusion, and resentment.
- **Replacement:** explicit legacy policy: commemorative, cosmetic, tradeable, bound, reissued, archived, or museumized.
- **Question:** will this scarcity become a story, a market distortion, or both?

## Data Sovereignty Collapse

The studio refuses to decide what information belongs in the client, so external tools become mandatory.

- **Bad default:** let community tools solve everything.
- **Damage:** elite gatekeeping, guide dependence, role failure, meta hardening, off-client opacity.
- **Replacement:** classify information into base UI, advanced UI, logs, APIs, hidden mystery, and automation limits.
- **Question:** is the game hard because the encounter is hard, or because required information lives outside the game?

## Returnability Dividend

Players trust a world more when they can leave and return without humiliation.

- **Bad default:** maximize daily/weekly obligation.
- **Damage:** burnout, missed-patch exile, friend incompatibility, chore walls.
- **Replacement:** catch-up, patch briefings, friend-sync, mentoring, old-content compression, account-wide continuity.
- **Question:** what happens to a loyal player who misses one patch?

## Prestige-Channel Contamination

Power, identity, money, achievement, profession, and trust share the same visual channel until no one knows what anything means.

- **Bad default:** use gear/cosmetics as universal signal.
- **Damage:** paid prestige confusion, unreadable combat, inspection gatekeeping, identity-role conflict, profession invisibility.
- **Replacement:** separate readiness, achievement, identity, social trust, monetization, profession history, tactical readability, and economic status.
- **Question:** can players tell the difference between power, skill, wealth, taste, craft, and trust?

## Institution Outsourcing

The real MMO exists in external tools because the client does not support the institutions it depends on.

- **Bad default:** ship thin institution UI and assume communities will fill gaps.
- **Damage:** the client cannot see, support, or measure the systems players actually rely on.
- **Replacement:** bring essential coordination, policy, role, event, recruitment, market, and learning surfaces into the client or official interfaces.
- **Question:** what must a serious player leave the client to do?

## Planetary Anonymity

A massive single world contains everyone but makes no one familiar.

- **Bad default:** assume no realms means stronger togetherness.
- **Damage:** infinite strangers, no local reputation, no preferred-stranger layer.
- **Replacement:** neighborhoods, routes, event cohorts, profession hubs, recurring objectives, leader networks, local markets, recent-ally memory.
- **Question:** what makes the same players cross paths again under meaningful conditions?

## Zerg Spectacle Collapse

A massive event becomes bodies, effects, and rewards without responsibility.

- **Bad default:** add bigger bosses and more participants.
- **Damage:** unreadable combat, leeching suspicion, no leaders, no local memory.
- **Replacement:** local jobs, role lanes, objective fronts, leader surfaces, contribution clarity, cohort memory, readable reward logic.
- **Question:** what is my job here, who helped me, and what changed because we came?

## Economy Trust Pollution

An economy fails socially before it fails mathematically.

- **Bad default:** treat price equilibrium as the whole economy.
- **Damage:** prestige doubt, merchant anonymity, bot-like markets, visible spam, honest effort feels foolish.
- **Replacement:** seller/crafter identity, market anomaly signals, commission history, visible enforcement of economy rules, prestige separation.
- **Question:** do players believe wealth, craft, and status are legitimate?

## Dependency Locality

A player becomes memorable because they provide a needed service in a named place or route.

- **Bad default:** treat interdependence as forced grouping or inconvenience.
- **Damage:** the game either punishes solo players or automates away every reason to know anyone.
- **Replacement:** create optional-but-valuable services: ports, guides, buffs, rez, rescue, pulling, scouting, crafting, hauling, camp replacement, market fulfillment.
- **Question:** which specific player becomes useful enough that I would remember or seek them again?

## Corpse-Run Translation

Danger matters when recovery creates rescue and retry; it fails when recovery becomes hostage punishment.

- **Bad default:** copy naked corpse runs, XP loss, or gear hostage penalties because they produced stories.
- **Damage:** players avoid risk, fall behind friends, or churn after loss.
- **Replacement:** recoverable danger, rescue boards, group recovery bonuses, route-risk recaps, temporary help-repairable penalties, and rescue recognition.
- **Question:** after death, does the system create a rescue story or an exit from the game?

## Camp Etiquette Surface

Public dungeons create culture only when the game gives players enough structure to negotiate shared space.

- **Bad default:** fully instance everything or leave all contested space to social chaos.
- **Damage:** sterile private content on one side, camp stealing/training/GM burden on the other.
- **Replacement:** named camps, spawn cadence, claim/sharing signals, replacement queues, train warnings, puller identity, local callouts, and repair tools.
- **Question:** what etiquette will players invent, and can the UI make the good version easier?

## Luclin Social-Substitution

A convenience feature replaces an emergent player-usefulness loop but fails to preserve the memory that loop carried.

- **Bad default:** centralize travel, trade, grouping, or recovery and measure only time saved.
- **Damage:** player services, regional hubs, route stories, merchant identity, and repeated contacts vanish.
- **Replacement:** convenience plus replacement memory: seller history, service reputation, last-mile routes, local hubs, commissions, recent allies, guide/porter roles.
- **Question:** what relationship did this convenience remove, and how does the replacement remember people?

## WoW Social-Residue Loss

A system solves access, speed, or population but deletes the names, places, roles, routes, guild needs, or prestige meanings that old friction accidentally produced.

- **Bad default:** judge modernization by minutes saved, completion rate, or population density.
- **Damage:** disposable strangers, hollow cities, guild optionality, world-as-pregame, and prestige confusion.
- **Replacement:** before removing friction, list the social residue it carried and ship replacement memory with the convenience.
- **Question:** what did the old inconvenience make players remember, and where does that memory live now?

## Access Without Social Aftercare

A queue, finder, teleport, or browser can find bodies but cannot by itself create relationships.

- **Bad default:** treat finding a group as the full social problem.
- **Damage:** strangers become content-fillers; role blame and leaving become cheap; learning has no continuity.
- **Replacement:** purpose tags, route expectations, story/learning/speed/farm/prog labels, recent allies, leader follow, play-again prompts, commendation memory, replay after failure.
- **Question:** after this run, can a good stranger become a preferred stranger?

## Difficulty-Mode Meaning Split

Multiple versions of the same content improve access but split the social meaning of the clear.

- **Bad default:** add more difficulties and let item level sort prestige.
- **Damage:** players “saw the raid” but did not join the same institution, learn the same norms, or share the same achievement meaning.
- **Replacement:** label each mode culturally: story access, learning, normal organized, heroic mastery, mythic prestige, seasonal push; keep rewards and history legible.
- **Question:** what does a clear mean, and can players tell which meaning they are seeing?

## Timer-Theft Pressure

A timed group objective turns one player’s mistake into everyone else’s lost time or downgraded reward.

- **Bad default:** add a timer for mastery and assume pressure creates skill.
- **Damage:** tanks/leaders absorb blame, off-meta players are filtered, learning becomes socially expensive, and leaving becomes rational.
- **Replacement:** declared push/completion/learning modes, route preview, key protection or non-hostage progression, post-failure replay scaffolds, role-burden transparency.
- **Question:** when someone fails, does the timer produce coaching, abandonment, or prosecution?

## World-Rewrite Memory Deletion

A patch modernizes old places but deletes the routes, jokes, landmarks, and personal history players used to anchor identity.

- **Bad default:** treat old content as obsolete content to replace.
- **Damage:** players experience improvement as loss; world continuity breaks; nostalgia becomes opposition to future change.
- **Replacement:** monuments, legacy names, optional history layers, veteran callbacks, preserved screenshots/maps, returning-player tours, explicit continuity framing.
- **Question:** what harmless memory can survive even if the mechanics must change?

## Token-to-Prestige Leakage

Money becomes gold, gold buys services, and services produce prestige-adjacent proof.

- **Bad default:** evaluate tokens only as RMT reduction or subscription flexibility.
- **Damage:** players distrust whether status, rare goods, carries, achievements, or economic power were earned.
- **Replacement:** separate purchased convenience, gold wealth, earned execution, non-transferable prestige, and social trust history.
- **Question:** can money reach a status channel players believe should be earned?

## Classic Nostalgia Misread

Players ask for an older game because they miss the social residue, not necessarily the inconvenience.

- **Bad default:** either dismiss nostalgia as irrational or copy old friction literally.
- **Damage:** modern design misses the real complaint, or reintroduces pain without the original community function.
- **Replacement:** identify which old loop created repeated faces, route memory, class usefulness, market talk, guild need, or prestige clarity; rebuild that function with modern UX.
- **Question:** what social function is nostalgia pointing at?

## FFXIV Pattern Addendum

### Trust Repair as World Memory

- **Bad default:** treat a failed live system as something to patch quietly.
- **Damage:** players remember the failure but the operator refuses to integrate the memory.
- **Replacement:** acknowledge the failure, preserve harmless identity, create a world/story bridge, and prove repair through cadence.

Diagnostic question: **What proof shows the operator learned?**

### Story Gate Debt

- **Bad default:** because story is good, make all meaningful play sit behind the full chronology.
- **Damage:** friends cannot play together tonight; newcomers experience story as exile from the current world.
- **Replacement:** preserve story-first play but add synced side paths, spoiler-safe current activities, recaps, and mentor-supported bridges.

Diagnostic question: **Can a behind player meaningfully play with current friends without destroying story memory?**

### Polite Automation

- **Bad default:** queue people together and assume low toxicity means good social design.
- **Damage:** players complete content peacefully but forget each other instantly.
- **Replacement:** add first-time signals, role clarity, commendations, recent allies, play-again prompts, and purpose-tag escalation.

Diagnostic question: **Did the queue create a remembered stranger or only a cleared instance?**

### Purpose-Tagged Obligation

- **Bad default:** let group intent be discovered in chat or after failure.
- **Damage:** players punish each other for mismatched expectations.
- **Replacement:** make purpose explicit before commitment: story, first-time, blind, learning, prog, clear, farm, speed, parse, social, venue.

Diagnostic question: **Which expectation would surprise someone only after the wipe?**

### Mode Separation

- **Bad default:** make one difficulty mode serve story, learning, farm, and prestige.
- **Damage:** incompatible motives collide; prestige either gatekeeps story or story access devalues prestige.
- **Replacement:** separate access, learning, mastery, and prestige channels.

Diagnostic question: **What social meaning does this mode carry?**

### Identity Outside Combat

- **Bad default:** combat performance is the main way to become known.
- **Damage:** social players become decoration; throughput and logs overdominate identity.
- **Replacement:** add glamour, portraits, housing, events, collections, crafting signatures, performance, screenshots, titles, and hosting identity.

Diagnostic question: **Can a player be known without being high-performance?**

### Exodus-Readiness

- **Bad default:** wait for a competitor to stumble, then market aggressively.
- **Damage:** skeptical visitors arrive and confirm that the world cannot onboard or absorb them.
- **Replacement:** build trust, onboarding runway, social warmth, comparison legibility, culture, and capacity response before the wave.

Diagnostic question: **Would a skeptical refugee’s first week prove or disprove your world’s promise?**

### Formula Fatigue

- **Bad default:** repeat the patch cadence that players trust until it becomes invisible.
- **Damage:** players respect the game but no longer feel surprise, need, or new social roles.
- **Replacement:** keep return rhythm but rotate social formats, institution needs, identity rewards, and world-state rituals.

Diagnostic question: **Is this a comforting ritual or a solved checklist?**
